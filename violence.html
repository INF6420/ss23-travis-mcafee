Public Disturbances and Violence Guidelines:

Public disturbances and acts of violence, such as fights, disruptive behavior, or harassment, can jeopardize the safety and well-being of library patrons, staff, and volunteers, as well as disrupt normal library operations. The following steps outline the recommended response procedure in the event of a public disturbance or act of violence:

 

  • Assess the situation: Quickly determine the nature and severity of the disturbance or violent act. Evaluate any immediate risks or hazards to patrons, staff, or volunteers.
  • Notify library staff: Alert other library staff members of the situation. They can assist in managing the disturbance and maintaining the safety of others.
  • Call 911 if necessary: If the disturbance escalates, involves a weapon, or poses an immediate danger to the safety of patrons, staff, or volunteers, call 911 to report the situation. Provide the library's address and a description of the incident. Follow any instructions provided by the dispatcher.
  • Implement de-escalation techniques: If it is safe to do so, trained library staff members should attempt to de-escalate the situation through verbal communication and non-threatening body language. Remain calm and professional, and avoid engaging in arguments or confrontations.
  • Evacuate or secure the area if needed: Depending on the severity and nature of the disturbance, it may be necessary to evacuate or secure the affected area of the library. Guide patrons, staff, and volunteers to a safe location away from the disturbance.
  • Cooperate with law enforcement and emergency responders: Work closely with law enforcement and emergency responders when they arrive. Provide them with any relevant information about the disturbance or violent act and follow their instructions.
  • Notify the library director and other library management: Inform the library director and designated emergency coordinator(s) of the situation as soon as it is safe to do so. They will coordinate any necessary follow-up actions and support the recovery of normal library operations.
  • Document the incident: After the public disturbance or act of violence is resolved and the situation is under control, document the details of the incident, including the nature of the disturbance, actions taken, and any injuries or property damage. This information will be used for incident reports and identifying areas for improvement in the library's emergency response procedures.
