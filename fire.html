Fire Emergencies:

Fire emergencies can occur for various reasons, such as electrical issues, faulty appliances, or hardware failure. Prompt and coordinated action is essential to protect the safety of library patrons, staff, and volunteers and minimize damage to the facility and its resources. The following steps outline the recommended response procedure in the event of a fire emergency:

 

  • Activate the fire alarm: If you discover a fire or see smoke, immediately activate the nearest fire alarm to alert occupants of the emergency and initiate evacuation procedures.
  • Call 911: Notify the fire department by calling 911. Provide the library's address, a description of the fire or smoke, and any other pertinent information.
  • Assess the situation: If the fire is small, and contained (e.g., a waste paper basket fire), and the source or cause is known, consider using a nearby fire extinguisher to put it out. However, prioritize personal safety and never attempt to fight a fire if it puts you or others at risk.
  • Evacuate the building: If the fire is too large to extinguish or presents a danger to occupants, follow the library's evacuation procedures. Library staff and volunteers should guide patrons to the nearest safe exit, assist individuals with disabilities, and ensure that everyone remains calm and orderly.
  • Account for all individuals: Once outside the building, gather at the designated assembly point and conduct a headcount to ensure that all patrons, staff, and volunteers have evacuated safely. Report any missing individuals to the fire department.
  • Do not re-enter the building: Wait for the fire department to extinguish the fire and declare the building safe before re-entering. Never re-enter a burning building or one filled with smoke.
  • Notify the library director and other library management: Inform the library director and designated emergency coordinator(s) of the situation as soon as it is safe to do so. They will coordinate with the fire department, assess damage, and determine the appropriate steps for recovery.
  • Document the incident: After the fire is extinguished and the situation is under control, document the details of the incident, including the cause, actions taken, and any injuries or property damage. This information will be used for insurance claims, incident reports, and identifying areas for improvement in the library's emergency response procedures.
