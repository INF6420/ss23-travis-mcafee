Utility Disruption Guidelines:

Utility disruptions, such as power outages, water main breaks, or loss of telecommunications services, can impact the normal operation of the library and pose challenges to the safety and comfort of patrons, staff, and volunteers. The following steps outline the recommended response procedure in the event of a utility disruption:

 

  • Assess the situation: Determine the nature and extent of the utility disruption. Identify the affected systems, such as electricity, water, or telecommunications, and evaluate any immediate risks or hazards.
  • Notify the appropriate utility provider: Contact the affected utility provider(s) to report the disruption and obtain information about the expected duration and any recommended actions.
  • Notify the library director and other library management: Notify the library director and designated emergency coordinator(s) of the situation, providing details about the disruption and any potential impacts on library operations.
  • Communicate with patrons, staff, and volunteers: Inform library patrons, staff, and volunteers of the utility disruption and any necessary precautions or adjustments to normal library operations.
  • Monitor the situation: Keep in contact with the utility provider(s) to receive updates on the status of the disruption and any estimated restoration times.
  • Adjust library operations as needed: Depending on the duration and impact of the utility disruption, it may be necessary to adjust library hours, services, or programming temporarily. Communicate any changes to patrons, staff, and volunteers.
  • Document the incident: After the utility disruption is resolved and normal library operations resume, document the details of the incident, including the type of disruption, actions taken, and any impacts on library operations or services. This information will be used for incident reports and identifying areas for improvement in the library's emergency response procedures.
