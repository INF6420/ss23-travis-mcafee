Severe Weather Event Guidelines:

Weather-related emergencies, such as tornadoes, severe thunderstorms, or blizzards, can pose significant risks to the safety of library patrons, staff, and volunteers, as well as the library facility itself. The following steps outline the recommended response procedure in the event of a weather-related emergency:

 

  • Monitor weather alerts: Stay informed of any severe weather warnings or watches issued by the National Weather Service or local authorities. Utilize weather alert apps, radios, or online resources to keep up to date on the latest conditions.
  • Notify patrons, staff, and volunteers: When a severe weather alert is issued, inform patrons, staff, and volunteers of the potential threat and any recommended precautions or actions.
  • Designate a safe area: Identify a safe area within the library where occupants can take shelter during a weather emergency. This area should be an interior room or hallway on the lowest floor of the building, away from windows and exterior walls.
  • Initiate sheltering procedures: If the weather situation becomes severe and poses a direct threat to the library, initiate sheltering procedures. Direct patrons, staff, and volunteers to the designated safe area, assist individuals with disabilities, and ensure that everyone remains calm and orderly.
  • Monitor weather conditions: While sheltering, continue to monitor weather updates and alerts. Stay informed of any changes in the storm's intensity or path and follow instructions from local authorities.
  • Wait for the all-clear signal: Do not leave the safe area until the severe weather has passed and local authorities have issued an all-clear signal. In some cases, this may require remaining in the safe area for an extended period.
  • Assess and address damages: Once the weather emergency has passed, assess the library for any damages or safety hazards. Notify the library director and emergency coordinator(s) of any issues, and coordinate with them to address immediate concerns and initiate recovery efforts.
  • Document the incident: After the weather emergency is over, document the details of the incident, including the type of weather event, actions taken, and any injuries or property damage. This information will be used for incident reports, insurance claims, and identifying areas for improvement in the library's emergency response procedures.
